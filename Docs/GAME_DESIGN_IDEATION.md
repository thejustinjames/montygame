# MontyGame — Game Design Ideation

**Working Title:** MontyGame  
**Target Audience:** Ages 5–7  
**Platforms:** Laptop (primary), iPad (future), Mac emulator  
**Initial Character Roster:** Dinosaur, Cat (custom-drawn)

---

## 1. High Concept

A charming **platformer-board hybrid** blending:
- **Board Game Progression** with thematic portals, whirlpools, elevators, and hyperspace jumps (not basic snakes/ladders)
- **Gentle, Exploratory Platforming** (forgiving, skill-based but not punishing; designed for 5–7 year-olds)
- **Space/Dinosaur Adventure** (flying rockets, dinosaurs, colorful sci-fi worlds)
- **Story-Driven Gameplay** (help Dino & Cat navigate through three worlds to escape/return home)
- **Multi-Faceted Learning** (counting, spatial reasoning, pattern recognition, turn-taking fairness)

Players navigate a multi-world board adventure where each tile is a mini-platforming challenge. Succeed → move forward; land on special tiles → activate portals, whirlpools, elevators, or hyperspace jumps. Win by reaching the goal and reuniting Dino & Cat with home.

---

## 2. Core Gameplay Loop

### Basic Turn (Single Player or Turn-Based Multiplayer)

1. **Roll the Die or Draw a Card:** Player taps to roll (1–6 movement) or draw a movement card (e.g., "Jump 3", "Dash 4", "Slow Step 2")
   - *Mix of both:* Roll-based on normal turns, card-draw on special "Lucky Spin" tiles
2. **Platforming Challenge:** Traverse the next tile(s) via a gentle mini-platforming section:
   - Dodge/avoid obstacles (rolling boulders, lasers, floating hazards—but forgiving hit boxes)
   - Optional power-ups scattered around (speed boost, shield, flight for 5 sec)
   - Jump over pits/gaps—but falling is not instant death; Dino/Cat bounce back and can retry
3. **Land & Check Tile Type:**
   - **Normal Tile:** Stay put; end turn
   - **Time Portal (↑ fast forward):** Warp forward +3 to +5 tiles (reward for good platforming)
   - **Whirlpool (↓ pull down):** Spin down -2 to -4 tiles (but not loss of life; just setback)
   - **Elevator (⬆ ascend):** Rise up +2 tiles (reaching higher levels on the board)
   - **Hyperspace Jump (→ warp):** Random teleport forward or sideways (exciting surprise)
   - **Mystery Tile (?):** Draw a random card effect (good or bad)
   - **Boss Tile:** Face a story-tied mini-boss; defeat to proceed (one per world)
4. **Turn Ends:** Multiplayer mode passes to next player; Solo mode continues to next turn

**Forgiving Design for Young Players:**
- No instant "game over" — falling into pits or hitting obstacles = gentle bounce-back or small penalty
- Encourages exploration and experimentation without fear

## 3. Story & Narrative Arc

### Main Story
**"Help Dino & Cat Return Home"**

Dino and Cat were playing in their cozy dino den when a mysterious portal opened, sucking them into a strange multi-world adventure! They now find themselves stranded across three worlds:
1. **Dino Jungle** — the lush, familiar green world where they first landed
2. **Space Station** — a glittering, high-tech world full of robots and aliens
3. **Rocket Canyon** — a desert world of flying rockets and ancient mysteries

**Goal:** Guide Dino and Cat through all three worlds, collect clues and power-ups, and reach the Final Portal to return home.

### Story Beats (Per World)
- **World 1 (Dino Jungle):** "We need to find the Portal Key! It's hidden at the end of this jungle."
- **World 2 (Space Station):** "A friendly robot tells us the next Key is on the other side of the station. Careful—there are laser traps!"
- **World 3 (Rocket Canyon):** "The final Key is guarded by an ancient rocket. We're almost home!"

### Ending
When players reach the goal on World 3, Dino and Cat reunite with their home and celebrate with a festive ending sequence.

## 4. Educational Elements (5–7 Year-Olds)

All four learning goals integrated across gameplay:

- **Counting/Math:** Roll the die, add up movement, track position ("Roll 4 + 2 power-up = land on tile 9")
- **Spatial Reasoning:** Jump gaps, navigate around obstacles, use portals/elevators to think vertically
- **Pattern Recognition:** Observe where whirlpools cluster (penalty zones), where portals lead (reward paths), predict board flow
- **Turn-Taking & Fairness:** Multiplayer mode teaches waiting for your turn, celebrating others' wins, learning from losses

Each world reinforces these through different obstacle types and puzzle patterns.

---

## 4. Game Modes

### Solo Mode
- Race against the clock or just explore
- Progressive difficulty (3 levels: Easy, Normal, Hard)
- Story: Help Dino & Cat escape a space station / return home

### Multiplayer Modes
- **Turn-Based (Local):** Pass controller between players; 2–4 players
- **Online (Future):** Async or real-time matches with simple matchmaking
- **Co-op (Future):** Both players work together to reach the goal

## 5. Level / World Structure

Three themed worlds, each with a 30–40 tile board:

### World 1: "Dino Jungle" 🦕
**Aesthetic:** Lush green jungle with vines, trees, and ancient ruins  
**Special Tiles:**
- **Time Portals (↑):** Warp forward +3–5 tiles (hidden behind waterfalls or in caves)
- **Whirlpools (↓):** Spin down -2–3 tiles (swampy vortexes; not dangerous, just setbacks)
- **Elevators (⬆):** Rise up to higher platforms +2 tiles (hanging from vines)
- **Hyperspace Jumps:** Random warp (through mysterious jungle fog)

**Obstacles:** Falling rocks, lava pits, swinging vines, slow-mud sections  
**Power-ups:** Dinosaur stomp (shake ground to clear rocks), sprint boost, vine-swing ability  
**Boss:** Giant T-Rex at world's end — requires pattern-dodging to pass (not fought head-on)  
**Learning Focus:** Introduction to basic movement, portal concept, turn-taking

### World 2: "Space Station" 🤖
**Aesthetic:** Sleek, high-tech blue/purple with robots, neon lights, zero-gravity vibes  
**Special Tiles:**
- **Time Portals (↑):** Teleport through warp gates +4–6 tiles
- **Whirlpools (↓):** Get pulled into air vortexes -2–3 tiles
- **Elevators (⬆):** Gravity lifts rise +3 tiles (between decks)
- **Hyperspace Jumps:** Quantum teleports (really fun/trippy visual)

**Obstacles:** Laser beams, floating debris, rotating platforms, force fields  
**Power-ups:** Flight mode (temporary), shield, slow-motion focus  
**Boss:** Friendly Robot Guardian (can't hurt it; must navigate its automated defenses via pattern recognition)  
**Learning Focus:** Spatial reasoning (3D space), prediction (laser patterns), reward collection

### World 3: "Rocket Canyon" 🚀
**Aesthetic:** Orange/red desert landscape with rocketing ships, sand dunes, ancient portals  
**Special Tiles:**
- **Time Portals (↑):** Rocket boosts forward +5–7 tiles (most powerful rewards!)
- **Whirlpools (↓):** Sand storms pull -2–4 tiles
- **Elevators (⬆):** Launch platforms rise +4 tiles (to upper canyon levels)
- **Hyperspace Jumps:** Teleport through ancient portals (mysterious!)

**Obstacles:** Spinning rockets (dodge!), sand storms, crumbling platforms, heat waves  
**Power-ups:** Rocket boost (extra fast), invincibility shield (brief), flight glide  
**Boss:** Rocket Boss — An ancient automated defense system; requires all learned skills to bypass  
**Learning Focus:** Mastery (combines all prior mechanics), endgame challenge, celebration

**Story Progression:** Complete World 1 → 2 → 3 → Final Portal → Return Home (ending sequence).

## 6. Character Abilities

### Dinosaur
- **Passive:** Slightly faster jump recovery; higher jump strength
- **Special:** Stomp ability — shake the ground (clears small obstacles, stuns enemies briefly)
- **Personality:** Cheerful, curious, loves exploring

### Cat
- **Passive:** Better air control; can change direction mid-jump
- **Special:** Pounce ability — dash forward quickly (dodge hazards or reach far tiles)
- **Personality:** Confident, playful, quick-witted

**Design Notes:** Different stats make each character feel unique but balanced. Kids can choose who they vibe with.

---

## 7. Multiplayer Specifics

### Turn-Based (Recommended for MVP)
- Players take turns spinning/rolling the die
- Shared board; everyone races the same path
- Leaderboard shows positions (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th)
- Game ends when first player reaches the goal tile

### Online (Stretch Goal)
- Async turn-based (player A plays, waits for player B)
- Simple lobby (invite friends via code, or auto-match)
- Replay system (watch replays of friends' games)

---

## 8. Educational Hook for Parents

> *"MontyGame blends learning and adventure: kids practice counting (dice rolls, tile positions), spatial reasoning (navigating platforming challenges), pattern recognition (predicting board flow), and social skills (turn-taking fairness). Story-driven and playful, designed for 5–7 year-olds. Each world is 10–15 minutes; the full adventure takes 30–45 minutes."*

## 10. MVP Scope (Phase 1)

To launch an early playable version:

1. **1 World + 20–25 tiles** (Dino Jungle world)
2. **2 Characters** (Dinosaur, Cat; both fully playable)
3. **Solo Mode:** Single-player campaign through Dino Jungle
4. **Multiplayer Mode:** Turn-based local multiplayer (2–4 players, same board, same world)
5. **Board Mechanics:** All four special tile types (portals, whirlpools, elevators, hyperspace jumps)
6. **Basic Platforming:** Jump, dodge, collect power-ups; gentle collision behavior (bounce-back, no death)
7. **Obstacles:** 4–5 types (pits, rolling rocks, vines, lava, mud)
8. **Boss:** One simple boss at end of world (pattern-dodging, non-violent)
9. **Power-ups:** 2–3 types (sprint boost, stomp/pounce, shield)
10. **UI:** Menu screen, character select, scoreboard (solo + multiplayer), progress tracker

**Est. Complexity:** Moderate-to-challenging. Requires platformer physics, board state management, multiplayer turn handling, and character sprite animation. Feasible with focused scope and chosen tech stack.

**MVP Deliverables:**
- Playable, feature-complete World 1
- Both game modes (solo + local multiplayer) functional
- Clean, kid-friendly UI
- 2–3 musical loops + sound effects
- All story scenes between worlds (brief intro, mid-game, ending)

## 11. Design Decisions (LOCKED IN)

✅ **Platforming Feel:** Gentle, forgiving, exploratory (no instant death; bouncing back on obstacles)  
✅ **Movement Mechanics:** Mix of dice rolls (1–6) + card draws for variety  
✅ **Solo + Multiplayer:** Both modes supported (solo campaign + local turn-based co-play)  
✅ **Learning Goals:** All four integrated (counting, spatial reasoning, pattern recognition, turn-taking fairness)  
✅ **Story:** Full narrative arc (Dino & Cat escape → journey through 3 worlds → return home)  
✅ **Special Tiles:** Thematic alternatives (Time Portals, Whirlpools, Elevators, Hyperspace Jumps)  
✅ **Aesthetics:** Bright, playful, hand-drawn style; inspired by child-drawn character art
